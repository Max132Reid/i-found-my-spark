00Prologue — the cycle
wordless
▼

No voiceover. No words. Just the rhythm of your life shown visually. Day — busy, movement, noise. Night — collapse, crying, slow music. Day again. Night again. Each loop slightly heavier than the last.

The mirror glitch lives here. Happy Max smiling. Glitch. Dark Max in the same position. Flicker back and forth. The film's entire conflict in one shot. The audience feels trapped in the cycle before you say a single word.

Shot capture list — film over 12 months
Mirror glitch — happy Max / dark Max, same position, same light. Film both versions separately, edit the flicker in post.
Baby footage — find old home videos or photos. Baby sleeping, baby not crying, baby content. The contrast shots depend on this.
Couch flashback — same couch, same position. One shot drawing or writing. One shot on phone. Match the framing exactly.
24-second day sequence — 1 second per hour. Sleep, phone, school, bus, food, lying around, doom scrolling. Shoot each hour as a distinct clip.
Chest cam world shot — camera strapped to chest, turning slowly in a busy place. You in focus, background in motion blur. Everyone on their phones.
The morning — phone on nightstand. Eyes open. Hand reaches for it before anything else. Screen lighting up your face.
Looking up at the sky — frustrated. Empty. Just you and the sky. Hold it longer than feels comfortable.
The mum scene — dark room, muffled audio, silhouettes, two figures, one moving closer. Film this when it feels right. Don't force it.
The quiet — you sitting somewhere with no phone. Just existing. Let it breathe longer than feels comfortable.
Making something again — hands drawing, writing, building. The spark returning. This shot might not exist until late in the year.
School captain / awards / people / places — capture these as they happen naturally. Don't stage them.
Final mirror — same mirror as prologue. No glitch. Just you. Hold it longer than you think you need to.

The hook — raw rap version of "I used to"
▼

You want to turn the "I used to" list into a short raw rap hook. Slow, mellow, spoken word feel.

You already have the collapse line: "Just when I realised I was shrinking, knowing I lost my spark."

Don't force it. When it comes, write it down immediately wherever you are.

Tone and music
Overall tone
▼

Quiet and intimate. Like talking to one person at 2am. Not motivational, not preachy. Cinematic and emotional. Short sentences with room to breathe. Long silences are not empty — they are part of the film. The audience should forget they're watching content.

Music direction
▼

Consider scoring this yourself under oncefinn. Deep, slow, mellow. The music should feel like it's coming from inside the film not playing over it.

Prologue: slow build, almost no melody. Act 2 low point: the heaviest moment, let music say what words don't. Act 4 ending: something that feels like exhaling. Bittersweet not triumphant.
